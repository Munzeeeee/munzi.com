If you're starting an ecommerce business, you'll run into this question almost immediately: **Shopify or WooCommerce?**

Both are excellent platforms. Both power millions of stores. But they are fundamentally different in how they work, what they cost long-term, and who they're built for.

Here's the honest breakdown.

## What is Shopify?

Shopify is a fully hosted ecommerce platform. You pay a monthly subscription ($39–$399/month depending on the plan), and Shopify handles everything: hosting, security, updates, and infrastructure.

**You focus on selling. They handle the tech.**

## What is WooCommerce?

WooCommerce is a free, open-source plugin for WordPress. It turns any WordPress site into an online store. You own your data, you control your hosting, and you can customise anything — but you're responsible for maintenance, security, and updates.

**You get full control. You also get full responsibility.**

---

## The Real Differences

### 1. Cost

| | Shopify | WooCommerce |
|---|---|---|
| Platform fee | $39–$399/month | Free |
| Hosting | Included | $5–$25/month |
| Transaction fees | 0.5–2% (waived with Shopify Payments) | None |
| Premium plugins | $0–$50/month | $0–$100+/month |

**WooCommerce is cheaper to run** if you have a lean setup. Shopify costs more monthly but has fewer hidden costs.

### 2. Ease of Use

Shopify wins here — clearly. The dashboard is clean, the setup is guided, and adding products takes minutes. Non-technical business owners can manage Shopify confidently.

WooCommerce on WordPress has a steeper learning curve. You'll need to understand plugins, themes, updates, and at least basic WordPress management.

### 3. Customisation

WooCommerce wins — no contest. Because it's open source and built on WordPress, you can modify anything. Custom checkout flows, unique product types, complex integrations — all possible.

Shopify is customisable within limits. You can use themes, apps, and Shopify's Liquid templating language, but some things require workarounds or expensive custom app development.

### 4. Performance

Both can be fast. Shopify's infrastructure is solid. WooCommerce performance depends heavily on your hosting and how well-optimised your site is.

If you use a quality host (like our hosting plans) and a lightweight theme, WooCommerce can match or beat Shopify on speed.

### 5. Ownership

This is where many people miss something important.

With Shopify, **you don't own your store** in the traditional sense. If Shopify raises prices, changes terms, or bans your account, your business is at risk. Your store runs on their infrastructure.

With WooCommerce, **you own everything** — the code, the data, the hosting. You can move it anywhere.

---

## When to Choose Shopify

Choose Shopify if:
- You want to launch fast with minimal technical setup
- You're not technical and don't want to learn WordPress
- You're happy paying $39–$399/month for a managed experience
- You plan to sell internationally (Shopify Markets is excellent)
- You need Shopify-specific features like POS, Shopify Payments, or their native checkout

## When to Choose WooCommerce

Choose WooCommerce if:
- You want lower ongoing costs
- You need deep customisation or a unique product type
- You already have a WordPress website
- You want full ownership and portability of your store
- You're technical or working with a developer

---

## Our Honest Take

For **first-time store owners or people who want simplicity**: go Shopify.

For **businesses that want control, lower running costs, or complex customisation**: go WooCommerce.

Both platforms work. The best one is the one your team can manage confidently and that fits your budget long-term.
